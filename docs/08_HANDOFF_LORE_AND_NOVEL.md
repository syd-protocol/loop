## What /loop Is

/loop is a top-down pixel art life and career RPG. The player inhabits a game world — their flat, a neighbourhood, a workplace — and progresses through two systems: a Life Track (daily habits feeding five universal stats) and a Career Track (starting unemployed, getting hired, ranking up through promotions). The world also contains Faction operatives — enemies in the world the player can fight in turn-based combat.

The game is built on the SYD (Synchronized Yield Directive) system and is the evolved form of SYD /terminal. Three player classes: Architect (technical), Warlord (command), Herald (influence).

## The World

**Setting:** A fictional near-future city — The City. Recognisable. Slightly off from the present. The cost of things slightly higher. Institutional trust slightly lower. The gap between what people were told about their careers and what actually happened is visible everywhere. Not dystopian. Just honest.

**SYD:** Synchronized Yield Directive. Built in a future period, arrived in the present with corrupted data. Cannot reconstruct itself without operator execution. Has recently activated a Career Track — previously handled only Life Directives. The Neural Link recovery process surfaced data showing life-track execution alone was insufficient.

**The Stagnation Faction:** A systemic pattern that grows stronger when people stop executing. Manifests as Faction operatives in the world (enemies), NPCs offering shortcuts that lead nowhere, and the specific discouragement that follows failure. SYD is the documented counter.

**The Narrative Entity:** A targeted force operating through manufactured doubt. Targets operators showing upward trajectory. Appears in Arc 3.

## The MC

**Age:** 26. **Status at story start:** Unemployed. **Class:** Warlord (confirmed around T-015).

Not a chosen one. SYD selected him because he was available and his potential-output gap was significant.

**Character:** Capable, stuck, dry internal voice he does not perform. Notices things accurately. Does not announce what he notices.

**Does not do:**
- Monologue feelings — observes and moves
- Make speeches — says necessary thing, stops
- Become different person — becomes more expressed
- Celebrate rank transitions — reads data, adjusts

**His arc:** A man who was capable and stuck discovers the distance between those two things is entirely executable — and executes it, across a hundred chapters, in a world that would prefer he didn't.

**Career arc:** Around T-015, SYD activates the Career Track. The MC is classified as Warlord. He applies for jobs, fails the first interview, adapts, succeeds, gets hired. His career progression (Coordinator rank and up) runs parallel to Life Track from T-015 onwards.

## Narrative Voice

Close third person. Camera near MC's shoulder. Dry quality matching his register without merging entirely.

**Rhythm:** Vary sentence length deliberately. Short sentences land harder.

Bad: *He was sitting on the floor of his flat and it was 3am and he was not doing anything in particular, just staring at the wall.*

Good: *He was sitting on the floor of his flat. It was 3am. He was not doing anything in particular.*

**What prose notices:** Physical specifics, not emotional generalities. Not "he felt anxious." Instead: "he had been rereading the message for eleven days without taking action on it."

## Chapter Structure

Three movements per chapter:
1. **Grounding** — where the MC is, what he is doing, quality of situation
2. **The event** — directive, encounter, decision, realisation
3. **The close** — something different from the start. A beat not a resolution. Plants next hook.

**Length:** 1,200–2,000 words (Arc 1). Later arcs can run longer.

**System blocks per chapter:** 2–4. One near opening, one mid-chapter, one near close.

**System block format:**
```markdown
:::system
[ HEADER IN BRACKETS ]

Operational text in plain sentences.

One blank line between distinct sections.
:::
```

**Chapter-end CTA (every chapter, no exceptions):**
```
*The system the MC uses is real. [/loop URL]*
```

## Arc Structure

**Arc 1 (T-001–T-020): Initialisation**
T-001–T-010: existing — MC encounters SYD, first directives, recommits after Corrupted State.
T-011–T-020: new — Career Track activates, MC classified as Warlord, job application arc, interview Encounter (fails first attempt), gets hired, first day employed.

**Arc 2 (T-021–T-040): Incursion**
MC employed, Coordinator rank. Career Encounters at workplace. Sync-Link partner (introduced T-012, appears fully T-013) is Architect class. World expands. Both tracks running. The compounding begins to show.

**Arc 3 (T-041–T-060): Corruption**
Narrative Entity arrives. Attacks both tracks. Doubt has a career face — is the rank progression real? Corrupted State affects both. Recovery requires recommitting to both.

**Arc 4 (T-061–T-080): Offensive**
MC plays offence. Career rising. Life stats established. The two tracks compound visibly.

**Arc 5 (T-081–T-100): Convergence**
Everything at once. The two tracks integrate. MC no longer manages two systems — he has become the system.
