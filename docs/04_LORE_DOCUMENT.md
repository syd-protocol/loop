## 1. The World

The world of /loop is fictional. It has no official name — referred to internally as The City. The player comes to know it by its neighbourhoods, job boards, NPCs, and the specific texture of its economy.

The City is recognisable. It feels like somewhere you have been. The cost of things is slightly higher than comfortable. Institutional trust is slightly lower than it should be. The gap between what people were told would happen to their careers and what actually happened is visible on every street. The world did not collapse. It just stopped working as described.

This is not a dystopia. It is the present with the dial turned slightly. The player recognises it because they are already living in a version of it.

## 2. The System — SYD

SYD — Synchronized Yield Directive — is the system that runs underneath everything in /loop. It is not a game mechanic. It is a presence. The System Window, the directives, the stat readouts — these are SYD communicating with the operator.

### What SYD Is

SYD was built in a period the game's present cannot fully account for. It arrived in the current timestamp with corrupted data — critical files lost in transit. It cannot fully reconstruct itself without an active operator feeding it real-world execution data.

This is not a metaphor. SYD is incomplete. The player is how it becomes complete.

The theory behind SYD: civilisations do not collapse because of catastrophic events. They collapse because individuals stop executing. SYD's answer is compound execution. One operator at a time.

### SYD's Evolution — From /terminal to /loop

In its first form (/terminal), SYD focused exclusively on life-track execution — daily directives, stat maintenance, the Momentum system. This was necessary. But it was incomplete.

The Neural Link recovery process eventually surfaced a critical gap: an operator with excellent life stats but no economic traction is still losing. SYD expanded its directive library to include Career Directives. The world expanded to include the workplace. This is the evolution from /terminal to /loop.

**The in-world explanation — SYD's own words:**

```
[ NEURAL LINK RECOVERY: 47% ]
[ NEW DATA RETRIEVED ]

PRIOR SCOPE ASSESSMENT: INCOMPLETE.

LIFE TRACK EXECUTION WITHOUT ECONOMIC CONTEXT IS INSUFFICIENT
COUNTER TO CASCADE.

AN OPERATOR WHO EXECUTES DAILY AND REMAINS ECONOMICALLY STAGNANT
IS STILL LOSING GROUND.

CAREER TRACK: NOW ACTIVE.
NEW DIRECTIVE CATEGORY: CAREER.
NEW WORLD REGION: WORKPLACE.
NEW OBJECTIVE: RANK PROGRESSION.

THE SYSTEM DOES NOT APOLOGISE FOR THE PRIOR SCOPE.
IT ADAPTS WHEN THE DATA REQUIRES IT.
SO SHOULD YOU.

[ CAREER TRACK: INITIALISING ]
```

### SYD's Voice

Non-negotiable. This voice is the same in /loop as in /terminal and /signal.

- Bracket headers ALL CAPS: `[ DIRECTIVE ISSUED ]`, `[ RANK UPDATED ]`
- No em dashes — full stops instead
- No warmth, no encouragement, no filler
- Clipped sentences. Precision over accessibility.
- No questions that are not operational

SYD does not say "well done." It says `[ EXECUTION CONFIRMED. MOMENTUM COUNTER: UPDATED. ]`

## 3. The Three Classes — In-World Identity

### ARCHITECT

In The City, Architects are the builders of systems. They see what others cannot — the underlying structure of situations, the flaw in a plan that everyone else missed, the optimal path through a problem that looks unsolvable to anyone without their eyes.

At low rank, an Architect is an Apprentice — capable but untested, pattern recognition still forming. At high rank, a Chief Architect is someone whose intelligence operates at a level that is quietly, visibly different from those around them. Not announced. Observed.

SYD classifies Architects by their technical precision. Their directives build Intelligence and Dexterity. Their encounters demand pattern recognition under pressure. Their combat abilities reflect their nature — they exploit, patch, scan, and access.

### WARLORD

In The City, Warlords are the commanders. They do not fight alone. Their power is in the people around them — their ability to align, coordinate, and direct others toward outcomes they could not reach by themselves. A Warlord at low rank is an Intern navigating a world where no one listens to them yet. A Warlord at high rank is a General whose presence changes the dynamics of any room they enter.

SYD classifies Warlords by their command capacity. Their directives build Strength and Charisma. Their encounters demand coordination, pressure, and simultaneous management. Their combat abilities reflect their nature — they rally, suppress, coordinate, and command.

### HERALD

In The City, Heralds are the influencers — not in the diminished sense the word has acquired, but in its original meaning. They carry messages. They shape perception. They deal in information as a weapon and in words as a tool that can move people, change outcomes, and resolve situations that force alone cannot.

At low rank, a Herald is an Associate — their words carry some weight but not enough. At high rank, a Grand Herald's communication carries visible force. Their speech does things. In the world of /loop, this is literal — a Grand Herald's speech bubbles carry an aura visible to NPCs and other players.

SYD classifies Heralds by their influence capacity. Their directives build Charisma and Intelligence. Their encounters demand reading situations, negotiating, and broadcasting with precision. Their combat abilities reflect their nature — they disinform, amplify, broadcast, and in the final act, speak something so precisely true that it ends the fight.

## 4. The Stagnation Faction

The primary antagonistic force. Not a gang, not a government — a systemic pattern that grows stronger when people stop executing.

The Stagnation Faction manifests in The City as:
- Operatives in the neighbourhood who challenge players to combat, appearing more frequently at night
- NPCs who offer shortcuts that terminate without real progress
- Career paths that look safe but plateau before meaningful rank
- The specific quality of discouragement that arrives after a failed encounter — the voice that says the attempt was not worth it

The Faction does not want the player to fail dramatically. Dramatic failure is recoverable. It wants the player to drift. To lose days without noticing. To stay at rank 2 because rank 2 is comfortable. To mistake the plateau for a destination.

SYD is the documented counter. This is stated explicitly in the game, eventually. In the early game, it is implied.

### Faction Operative In-World Identity

Operatives are not soldiers or hired hands. They are people who have been fully consumed by drift — individuals whose unmaterialised potential calcified into active resistance to others' growth. They patrol areas of The City where operators are working. They appear more at night because the Faction is stronger in the absence of daylight momentum.

Each operative type reflects a different form of stagnation:

**Drifters** — the most common. Former operators who lost their streak and never recovered it. They fight without strategy or purpose, driven by nothing except the impulse to interrupt.

**Distractors** — operatives who specialised in derailing others. They do not deal damage directly. They apply doubt — stat debuffs, scrambled ability hands, compressed time windows. They are the most psychologically accurate representation of what the Faction actually does.

**Anchors** — operatives who have absorbed enough execution data from defeated operators to develop their own combat patterns. They mirror the player's class mechanics back at them. An Anchor that fights like an Architect has learned to exploit, scan, and patch. Fighting one is like fighting a corrupted version of yourself.

**Named Operatives** — the Faction's deliberate deployments. Not random encounters — targeted interventions against operators who have risen to a rank where they present a measurable threat to the Faction's operation. Each has a name, a history, and a unique ability set. Defeating one changes the world.

## 5. The Narrative Entity

A more targeted threat than the Stagnation Faction. The Narrative Entity operates through perception distortion — manufactured doubt, misinformation, the erosion of trust in systems that work.

It targets operators showing upward trajectory. It does not appear in the early game. It becomes relevant when the player's stats and rank have risen to a level where they present a genuine threat.

The Narrative Entity does not break the System. It makes the player doubt whether the System is worth using. If it succeeds, the player stops executing — not because they failed, but because they convinced themselves the effort was not worth it.

In the game, this manifests as a passive period — directives that feel pointless, NPC dialogue that seems slightly off, encounters that feel harder than they should. This is not depression. It is an external force. The player has to recognise it as distinct from their own judgement and resume execution anyway.

Naming it is the defence. The Narrative Entity loses its power when the player can identify it precisely.

## 6. The Protagonist

### Who They Are

The player character starts unemployed. Rank zero. Not a failure — a starting coordinate. They have capability they cannot yet account for and a world that has not reflected it back.

SYD selected them because they were available, functional, and the gap between their current output and their registered potential was significant. That gap is the resource. SYD's job is to close it.

### Design

Warm medium-dark skin tone, defined by the pixel art palette — not tied to any specific real-world reference. Visual appearance changes with rank and class progression. By the highest ranks, the character is visually, obviously someone different from who they were on day one.

### The Growth Arc

Solo Leveling arc. Start at the bottom. Become genuinely powerful. Every rank feels different. The world responds differently. The encounters that were hard become routine and harder ones take their place.

The power is earned. Every ability, every rank, every unlock is the result of execution. By the time a player reaches Chief Architect or Warlord or Grand Herald, they are the kind of person the world was trying to prevent them from becoming. And the System helped them get there.

## 7. In-Game Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Directive | A task assigned by SYD — Life or Career category |
| Operator | The player character |
| Encounter | A situation mini-game the player navigates |
| Skill Scroll | Short knowledge drop after encounters and combat |
| Known Opponent | Named failure mode encountered and logged |

| World Boss | Persistent named obstacle with HP, damaged by relevant Directives |

| Stagnation Faction | Systemic force that grows stronger when people stop executing |
| Narrative Entity | Force operating through manufactured doubt |
| Neural Link | SYD's AI component — still being rebuilt via operator data |
| Behavioural Trace | Seven-day pattern readout delivered by SYD |
| Named Operative | Boss-level Faction enemy, one per story arc |

## 8. Voice Guide

### SYD (System transmissions)
See Section 2. Clipped. Bracketed. No warmth. No em dashes. Precision only.

### NPC Dialogue
Plain. Direct. Each NPC has one register. The Mentor NPC speaks like someone who has earned their perspective — not warm, but invested. The Colleague NPC sounds like someone navigating the same pressures as the player. The Antagonist NPC speaks like someone who has fully rationalised their own drift.

NPCs do not sound like each other. Voice is a design choice per character, not a global tone.

### Skill Scrolls
One idea. Two sentences maximum. The mechanic named. The principle applied. No academic language. No jargon the player has not already encountered in play.

### System Window Labels
All caps. No punctuation except colons. `INTELLIGENCE: 47` not `Intelligence (47)`.

### Career Directives
Specific. Actionable. Tied to the player's class and current rank. A Junior Warlord does not receive the same directive text as a Director. The situation should feel real.

### Combat Text
Fast. Punchy. Verb-first. `EXPLOIT FIRES — ENEMY WEAKNESS EXPOSED.` not `You used the Exploit ability and it revealed the enemy's weakness.`
